Greek Culture

Rich History & Traditions

Greece has a rich and influential history that spans thousands of years, shaping much of Western civilization through its contributions to philosophy, democracy, art, and science. From the ancient city-states of Athens and Sparta to the intellectual legacy of Plato and Aristotle, Greece’s classical era laid the foundations for modern thought. Later, as part of the Roman and Byzantine Empires, Greece preserved its language, Orthodox Christian faith, and cultural identity through centuries of change. Today, Greek traditions reflect this deep historical heritage. Name days are widely celebrated, often more than birthdays, with gatherings, food, and well-wishes. Greek Easter is the most important religious event, featuring candlelit church services, red-dyed eggs, and traditional feasts. Weddings and baptisms are vibrant, family-centered events filled with symbolic rituals and joyful music and dancing. The Orthodox Church remains central to both spiritual life and national identity. Seasonal festivals like Apokries (Carnival) bring communities together with costumes and parades rooted in ancient celebrations. Hospitality, or filoxenia, is a core Greek value expressed through the sharing of food, especially meze and ouzo. National holidays such as March 25 (Independence Day) and October 28 (Ohi Day) commemorate the country’s resilience and pride, tying modern Greece to its storied past.

Greek Cuisine

Delicious Flavors

Greek cuisine is characterized by fresh, vibrant ingredients such as olive oil, herbs, vegetables, and seafood. Dishes like moussaka, souvlaki, and tzatziki are favorites among locals and visitors alike.
